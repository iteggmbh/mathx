URLs for individual Curves

  • ecc.html#a=1&b=-1 Weierstrass Curve used by the standardized ECDSA Signature Scheme. The parameters a and b may be chosen in a range from -1 to 1, a is most often shosen as -1, while the most prominent choices for b are 0, 0.3, 0.4 or 1.
  • ecc.html#d=10 Edwards Curve used by the Ed25519 Signature Scheme. The parameter d may be chosen in a wide range from -100 to 100. The additional parameter c mentioned by the references is chosen to be one.
Examples: #a=-1&b=0, #a=-1&b=1, #d=10 or #d=-10.

Interactive Usage

  • Ctrl-Click or long touch (0.5-1s) selects the first point p of the addition, a second selection selects the second point q and you will earn the resulting point p+q as a third point.
  • Ctrl-Shift-Click or extra long touch (>1s) selects one point p for doubling and you will earn the resulting point 2·p as a third point.
For the Weierstrass case you will also see the defining plane aka the line in the projective space intersecting the curve at the mirrored point of p+q.

History

Around Christmas 2015 I played around with 3D visualizations together with my son and stepped over the three.js library, which suddenly trapped my attention as a mathematician and long-time developer in computer graphics.

On the other side, I'm also involved in consulting large customers on cryptography topics, so I soon decided to visualize the group operation used by Elliptic Curve Craptography schemes in the Projective Space represented by the semi-shpere you see on teh canvas.

A numerical integrator and an implementation of Cardano's formula later I was able to draw the classical Weierstrass Elliptic Curves, which constitute the standard ECDSA algorithm together with the group peration defined on it.

Being advised by Robert Penz, a fellow seucrity specialist in our area, I suddenly started implementing Edwards Curves, which have a much simpler group operation as you can prove by reading the code in ecc.js
